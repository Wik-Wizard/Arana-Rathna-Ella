Road To Rathna Ella

18 March, 2022 · History

On the western slope of the central mountain range, at the bottom of the magnificent 18 bends you will find the town of Hasalaka, the birthplace of a hero. Traveling towards Hettipola from the statue of the hero, Gamini Kularathne you can reach Mahaessuma village and then Rathna Ella village. You have to park the vehicles in the parking lot and go on foot to see the rathna ella falls and Kaluwawatunu falls.in the village you can see a number of industries which are inherited to the area. There are about 284 families living in Ratna Ella with a social background based on Dumbara handicrafts, pottery, mining and agriculture. You can take the canal in the Knuckles Reserve Zone which starts at the end of the village passing the picturesque paddy field, cultivated in “helamalu” method...The Ging River, which starts from the Knuckles peaks, which covers about 343 hectares like the Meemure rock, forms several waterfalls, feeds several villages and joins the Mahaweli River up to Hasalaka Oya. Kaluwawatunu Ella can be seen from about 600 m below the Ratna Ella waterfall. The waterfall is about 15m high and 25m wide and has a breathtaking view to the eye. It is also saying that Sita Devi came to this place for bathing from the hidden palace across the Hasalaka stream through a tunnel. then walking about 200m on the same road again, you will come across the anicut built by Mr. H.M Navarathne around 1978 to carry water for all the needs of the village of Ratna Ella. Even today, one can see the homage paid to the god “Kele Bandara” before getting water for farming. A few meters away from here, one can see the Ratna Ella waterfall. It is about 113m in height, 78m in width and withal it is the sixth tallest waterfall in Sri Lanka.

Bird species endemic to Sri Lanka can be seen in the vicinity of this reserve such as vultures, hawksbills as well as lizards and snakes. Endemic Plants such as ebony, the cork-leaved bayur and agarwood are also found. It is the responsibility of all of us to protect this valuable resource, which is of great value and consistent with biodiversity.

Coming here and leaving only footprints and carrying only memories is a great service to our future as well as to the commitment of the villagers who are striving to preserve this resource

Other places you have to visit around Rathna Ella

2 Februar, 2022· Photography

There are more than 15 places you can visit around RathnaElla. In this blog we briefly introduce few major places you can visit around Rathna Ella. Mahiyanganaya is the nearest tourist destination among them. In Mahiyanganaya you can visit,


Mahiyanganaya Temple

Mahiyanganaya temple is an ancient temple. According to written history Mahiyanganaya was the destination of lord buddha's third visit to sri lanka. After few centuries the Mahiyanganaya pagoda was built in king...Dutugamunu era (Dushta gamini abhaya) . Today more than hundred thousand local and foreign visitors annually visit Mahiyanganaya temple. According to Buddhists this temple is a major religious place

Dabana Veddas’ village

Sri Lankan aborigines (Sri Lankan native people) known as “Vedda”. In ancient times they hunted animals using arrows, but they are also a friendly human group. They also fulfilled with humanity. Our aborigines have an own culture. Most speak Sinhala instead of their indigenous languages, which are alsmost dead languages. Talk with them and stay a night with them gives you an exciting experience

Yahangala rock

Yahangala is another tourist destination for you. “Yahan” means the bed. “Gala” means the rock. So Yahangala means the rock like a bed. Most of visitors like to get the thrilling and exciting hiking experience in Yahangala

Malgahawela pathana

If you visit Yahangala you can also see the malgahawela pathhana. Villagers said that there are more wild elephants than that in pathana(plains). Most of visitors were amazed when they saw that pathana(plains).

18 bend road

Daha ata wanguwa(18 bend road) is a famouse road laid in the central province of Sri Lanka,connecting the cities Kandy and Mahiyanaganaya .The road is 41km long.As a part of the A26 road it has been improved through years. It's cosidered as one of the challenging and difficult road by many drivers in the country.

Remote villages

When you visit Rathna Ella you can also visit rural remote traditional village like Udagaldebokka, Udaththawa, Galamuduna etc. The main occupation of the villagers is farming. Those villages are rich in biodiversity In this blog we mentioned few places only. But you can also visit more than this. So why are you being late.
